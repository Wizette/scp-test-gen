Question 59 of 63

One Gemara in Brachos teaches that juice from a fruit is shehakol and another teaches that broth from vegetables is ha’adama. The Rishonim ask, how could the juice itself be downgraded to a shehakol and the broth (which is just water) upgraded to a ha’adama.

a.      What are two ways to reconcile this contraction?

b.      How does the S”A passkin?
Answer given:
a. Juice is viewed as sweat of fruit/veg, broth is ממשות of fruit/veg
רשב"א: Determined by NORMALLY cooked/juiced. So, fruits/veg normally cooked retain Bracha, otherwise Shehakol
רא"ש: Determined by cooked or not cooked. So, fruits/veg that are cooked retain Bracha, otherwise Shehakol

b. Where the opinions agree, retain Bracha, where disagree Shehakol

What are the three categories of Ikar vetafel?

Feedback:

There are three main scenarios of ikar v’tafel mixtures:

1. טפל המלפת – the tafel enhances the ikar. In this situation, the tafel is usually not eaten alone, but is eaten together with the ikar and enhances the flavor of the ikar. For example, cake with whipped cream, French fries with ketchup or a cracker with cream cheese.

2. טפל המשמש – the tafel services the ikar. In this situation, the tafel is not eaten together with the ikar and is only consumed to offset the taste of the ikar that was eaten first. For example, if you eat a hot pepper and need milk to alleviate the spiciness of the pepper or if you are trying herring for the first time and you can’t stand its flavor and texture so you quickly eat a cracker, the halacha is that you do not recite a bracha on the tafel even though it is eaten alone and after the ikar.

3. טפל המעורב – the tafel is mixed with the ikar. In this mixture both foods would be eaten alone and one is not clearly enhancing the other. For example, a fruit salad – where we follow the majority.

a. The one exception to this rule is when there is mezonos in the mixture. As we learned above in סימן רח-ב, when mezonos is added to a mixture to enhance the flavor or to be filling, the bracha on the mixture is “mezonos” even if the wheat is the minority of the mixture.
